Ed Lingsch

Ed was born in San Francisco on December 26, 1913 and passed away on November 12, 2011 at the age of 97 after developing Alzheimer’s Disease. He was a graduate of Cogswell Polytechnical College and longtime member of the Italian American Social Club of San Francisco. In 1952 he founded San Francisco real estate company Lingsch Realty where he worked until he retired at the age of 94. For his last 49 years, he resided in Millbrae. Ed was a loving and generous father and grandfather who always had a joke to tell and a smile on his face. He will be remembered for his sense of humor, his strong work ethic, and his love of poker and the track.

Golden Gate Fields

Self-described as the place “Where the bay comes to play,” San Francisco’s historic Golden Gate Fields is a beautiful facility that features an elegant Turf Club, a vast and comfortable Club House, and smart simulcast rooms. On top of the always-thrilling horse racing action, guests are treated to panoramic views of the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and the San Francisco skyline.

The Alzheimer's Association

The Alzheimer’s Association is the world’s leading voluntary health organization in Alzheimer’s care, support and research. The Association works on global, national and local levels to enhance care and support for all those affected by Alzheimer’s and other dementias. As the largest non-profit funder of Alzheimer's research, the Association is committed to accelerating progress of new treatments, preventions and ultimately, a cure.
